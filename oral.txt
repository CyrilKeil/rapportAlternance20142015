-Introduction anglais

Hi, thanks for coming to my presentation. Today, I'm gonna present you what I did during all this year at the Genoscope.
I began my mission on the 3rd of november 2014. My mission was to work on a sub-project of our main project NGL. NGL stands for
Next Generation LIMS. LIMS is translated by "Laboratory Information Management System". Basically, it's an automatised management system for
data generated in laboratory.
We already had a LIMS at the Genoscope, but considering its age, it was to difficult to maintain it over the time, and the decision to create a
new LIMS, based on new generation technologies, has been taken. This decision has been taken three years ago, and some projects, like the 
one I was working on, is a soon-to-be finished project.
After a week reading documentation, trying to make up for my lack of knowledge in javascript, i started to work on the sub-project named NGL-BI.
It's an application designed to consult data generated from genome's sequencing.
If you don't know what genome sequencing is, I'm gonna explain it quickly.

Sequencing is the process of determining the nucleotide order of a given fragment of DNA. The sequence of DNA encodes the necessary information 
for living things to survive and reproduce. Contrary to the human genome, we still haven't sequenced most of the plants' genome. Therefore, the
main objective at the Genoscope is to sequence the genome of most plants, to understand how they work, and what nucleotide order give a particular
trait to the plant.
